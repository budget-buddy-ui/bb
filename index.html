1. Allowances and Spending Plans

What Is an Allowance?

An allowance is money you regularly receive from your parents or guardians. You might get it every week or month, often for doing chores or learning how to manage money responsibly.

What Is a Spending Plan?

A spending plan, or budget, is a guide that shows how much money you have and how you plan to use it. It helps make sure your money doesn’t run out too quickly.

Why Are Spending Plans Important?

  • They help balance income (the money you get) and expenses (the money you spend).
  • They guide you to make better choices.
  • They allow you to plan for savings and donations.

The Three Main Categories in a Spending Plan:

  • Spend – Use money for items you want or need now (toys, food, clothes).
  • Save – Set money aside for later or bigger purchases.
  • Give – Donate to others or help people in need.

2. Being Responsible With Money

What Does It Mean to Be Responsible With Money?

Being money-responsible means knowing:

  • How much money you have.
  • Where your money goes.
  • How to avoid spending more than you earn.

Why Should You Keep Track of Spending?

Keeping records of your spending helps you:

  • Stay within your plan.
  • See where your money went.
  • Avoid forgetting or guessing your expenses.

You can use:

  • Receipts to remember what you bought.
  • Notebooks or diaries to write down your spending.
  • Envelopes or folders to keep money and records organized.

What Happens If You Overspend?

If you spend more than you have:

  • You might not have enough for other important needs.
  • You may miss opportunities to buy something more useful later.
  • You could develop bad habits that cause problems when you grow up.

3. Saving and Investing

What Is Saving?

Saving means keeping some of your money instead of spending it right away. You can save:

  • In a piggy bank or jar.
  • In a savings account at a bank.
  • For short-term goals (like toys).
  • For long-term goals (like a bike or a trip).

What Is Interest?

When you put money in a savings account, the bank adds extra money called interest. The longer your money stays, and the higher the interest rate, the more it grows.

Example:

  • If you save 100 php at 10% interest per year:
  • After 1 year: 110 php
  • After 3 years: 130 php
  • After 10 years: 200 php

What Is Investing?

Investing means putting money into things that can grow in value, like stocks. A stock is a tiny piece of a company. If the company does well, your stock can become more valuable.

4. Comparison Shopping

What Is Comparison Shopping?

Comparison shopping means checking different options before buying something. It helps you get the best value for your money.

Why Is It Important?

  • Helps avoid wasting money.
  • Teaches you to spend wisely.
  • Protects you from tricky advertisements.

Needs vs. Wants

  • A need is something you must have (like food, water, shelter, school supplies).
  • A want is something nice to have but not necessary (like candy, toys, or video games).

Tip: Needs come first when you spend money.

How Do Advertisements Affect You?

  • Ads use colors, sounds, and catchy words to make products look more exciting than they are.
  • Sometimes ads make you think you need something you really don’t.

Be smart—ask: “Do I really need this?” before buying.

Making Smart Shopping Choices:

  • Check different brands and prices.
  • Read labels and features.
  • Don’t believe everything in ads—look for facts, not just opinions.
  • Choose the best item based on quality, price, and usefulness—not popularity.
